# Compiling Projects

Writing a project means working across many separate documents. **Compiling** brings them back together — pulling your work into a single, continuous output you can read, share, or export.

You can compile a project two ways:

- From the [Start Page](start-page.md), without opening the project.
- From within the project itself, once you're working in it.

Before compiling you choose which documents to include and the output format — plain text, PDF, or Word (`.docx`). The text documents are stitched together in the order of your [resource tree](resource-tree.md), so they come out in the sequence you arranged them. When a draft is ready to hand off — to a reader, an editor, or just yourself for a full read-through — compiling assembles the finished whole from its parts.
