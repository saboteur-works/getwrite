# Resources & Resource Types

A **resource** is any item inside your project — a document, an image, an audio clip, or a folder. Each resource has a stable identity that stays the same even if you rename it or move it to another folder, so links and references to it never break.

Keeping different kinds of material together as resources means your reference images, recordings, and notes can live right alongside the writing they support.

## Resource types

- **Text** — a rich-text document you edit in the [editor](editor/headings.md). The heart of most projects.
- **Image** — shown in the Work Area, with its resolution and EXIF details available in the metadata panel. Useful for reference art, maps, mood boards, and the like.
- **Audio** — played with a built-in play/pause/scrub widget; its duration and format appear in the metadata. Handy for recorded notes, interviews, or song demos.
- **Folder** — groups other resources together. Selecting a folder opens its contents in the Work Area, letting you work at the section or chapter level.

## Metadata and history

Every resource can carry [metadata](metadata.md) — notes, status, story dates, and more — and text resources keep a full [revision history](revisions.md) so earlier drafts are never lost.
